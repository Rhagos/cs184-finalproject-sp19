Final Project Proposal

Title: Gun Range Simulation Summary: This VR application brings players to a simulated gun range where they can use a variety of firearms with several different attachments. Players can customize the range environment, targets, and other parameters to suit their preferences and needs. Team members: Benjamin Wu, Daniel Wu, Charles Hager Problem Description Firearm training is inaccessible to some people due to factors such as location, cost, hassle, and safety, all of which is compounded for novice shooters. A virtual reality gun range is a great first step for those who want to learn to handle firearms, as it is a risk-free and beginner-friendly environment to practice. Goals and Deliverables Interactive demo: Player starts at a gun range with firearms on a table in front of them, and targets at various distances behind the table. The player can pick up, interact with the operations of, and fire the guns. Minimum requirements: Optical attachments for certain guns Magnifying sight for hunting rifle Holographic sight for semi-automatic rifle Basic gun range environment map Flat terrain Targets Table Skybox and map borders Basic gun sounds Basic bullet raycasting Basic gun functionality and animations: Physics - gravity, collisions Releasing and reloading magazines Pulling the slide back and cycling a bullet One of each major type of consumer firearm: 1 semi-automatic pistol 1 pump-action hunting shotgun 1 bolt-action hunting rifle 1 semi-automatic rifle Pure graphics components: Optical sights Holographic sight: mimicking the “infinity effect” transmission hologram Magnifying sight: second camera rendering with higher FoV, or custom magnifying shader Inverse kinematic arms for
